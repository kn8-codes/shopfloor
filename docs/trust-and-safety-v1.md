# ShopFloor trust and safety v1

## Goal
Set the minimum trust/safety posture for MVP without turning the product into bureaucratic sludge.

## Core principle
ShopFloor is for practical help between real local people.
That means trust matters, but overbuilt verification too early will kill the product.

So v1 should aim for:
- enough safety to reduce obvious harm
- enough simplicity to keep the product alive

---

## What trust comes from in v1
Trust should come from visible useful activity:
- completed help count
- field notes contributed
- neighborhood consistency
- response history

Not from:
- star ratings
- follower counts
- fake reputation theater

---

## Public vs private posture
### Public-ish by default
- request title
- category
- neighborhood
- shop card basics
- field notes

### Not public by default
- exact address
- phone number
- personal family details
- sensitive housing/legal details
- kid-specific identifying info

---

## MVP safety rules
1. no exact addresses in public posts
2. no money transfer system in MVP
3. no child-care matching as a headline use case yet
4. no crisis/emergency promise language
5. allow users to flag unsafe, scammy, or abusive behavior
6. allow request deletion / closure by author
7. keep moderation manual and lightweight at first

---

## Categories to be careful with
High-risk categories that may need softer launch rules:
- childcare pinch help
- rides for vulnerable users
- housing/legal disputes involving landlords
- domestic conflict or unsafe-home situations

These can exist later, but MVP should not pretend to safely operationalize them without policy and moderation thought.

---

## Recommended MVP moderation stance
Manual stewardship over automation.

Need:
- flag button
- basic admin review queue later
- ability to hide request/profile if needed
- basic house rules in plain language

---

## House rule draft
- be local
- be useful
- do not lie about what you can do
- do not post exact addresses publicly
- do not harass, scam, or pressure people
- leave the place more useful than you found it

---

## Bottom line
V1 safety should protect against obvious harm while preserving the core feeling:
this is a practical neighborhood tool, not a corporate platform pretending to care.
