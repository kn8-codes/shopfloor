# Multimeter

## What it is

A multimeter is a small battery-powered device with a screen, a dial, and two probes. It measures electrical properties in a circuit or component.

Most handheld meters can read:

- **DC voltage** — batteries, power supplies
- **AC voltage** — wall outlets (with caution)
- **Current** — flow through a circuit
- **Resistance** — continuity and component checks
- **Continuity** — beeps when two points are electrically connected

## What it is used for

- Checking if a battery is dead or a power supply is outputting the right voltage
- Finding broken wires or cold solder joints (continuity test)
- Verifying a fuse is blown
- Measuring current draw of a device
- Confirming a circuit is de-energized before touching it

## Beginner-safe first use

1. **Set the dial to the right mode.** For batteries and DC power, choose DC voltage (V⎓). Start on the highest range or use auto-range if available.
2. **Plug the black probe into COM and the red probe into VΩmA.**
3. **Touch the probes to the two points you want to measure.** For a battery, red to positive (+), black to negative (−).
4. **Read the display.** If the number is negative, your probes are reversed; that is safe, just swap them.
5. **For continuity**, set the dial to the continuity symbol (usually a sound wave or diode symbol), touch the probes together to hear the beep, then probe the two ends of a wire or fuse.

## Safety / stop conditions

- **Never measure current (A) across a power source.** This creates a short circuit. Current must be measured in series with the load.
- **Do not touch probe tips to live mains (wall outlets) unless you are trained and the meter is rated for it.** Most cheap meters are not safely rated for mains work.
- **If the meter shows "OL" or "1___",** you are exceeding the selected range. Move to a higher range or check your connections.
- **Stop if anything gets hot, smells, or sparks.** Disconnect power immediately.
- **Replace the battery** if readings drift or the display dims; a weak battery causes false readings.
