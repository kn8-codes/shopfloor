# Privacy-first handling

Privacy-first handling means the helper only looks at what is needed to help, and only with consent.

This matters for phones, computers, forms, accounts, photos, documents, benefits paperwork, medical paperwork, and anything else that can expose a person’s life.

## Basic rules

- Ask what the person wants recovered, fixed, or understood.
- Explain what you need to look at before looking.
- Do not browse unrelated files, photos, messages, or accounts.
- Do not copy personal data unless the person understands where it is going.
- Do not keep extra copies.
- Do not turn someone’s problem into public content.

## Good helper language

“I only need to see the charging screen.”

“I do not need to open your photos.”

“If we copy this data, where do you want it stored?”

“I’m going to stop here because the next step could risk the data.”
