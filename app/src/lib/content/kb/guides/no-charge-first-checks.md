# No-charge first checks

## What problem this helps with

Use this when a phone, tablet, handheld device, or small electronics item will not charge or will not turn on.

This guide does not prove the device is repairable. It helps rule out the easy things first.

## Quick triage

1. Try a known-good charger and cable.
2. Check the charging port for lint, bent pins, looseness, or a broken center tongue.
3. Leave the device on a known-good charger for 20–30 minutes if the battery may be deeply discharged.
4. Try a different outlet.
5. If safe, inspect for swelling, heat, liquid damage, cracked glass, or burnt smell.

## Tools and materials

- Bright light
- Magnification if available
- Wooden toothpick or plastic pick for lint removal
- [[Multimeter]] for basic charger or cable checks
- Known-good cable and charger

## Safety / stop conditions

Stop if the device gets hot, smells burnt, shows battery swelling, has visible liquid inside, or sparks. Do not keep plugging it in.

Do not scrape a charging port with metal tools. It can short pins or damage the connector.

Do not heat a device with an installed lithium battery.

## What to try first

Start with the cable and charger. A surprising number of no-charge problems are cable problems wearing a better costume.

If the cable is known-good and the port feels loose, inspect the [[USB-C receptacle]]. If the port is physically broken, stop and treat it as a repair job, not a cleaning job.

If the port looks packed with lint, remove power and gently loosen debris with a non-metallic pick. Do not force anything.

## When to ask for experienced help

Ask for experienced help if the port is broken, the battery may be swollen, the device contains important data, or the device still will not charge after the simple checks.
